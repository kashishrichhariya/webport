Rivaah
A space that invites stillness

Spatial & experience design for premium bridal jewellery retail

Year of Completion

Key Contributions
Lead Spatial Designer — material & colour palette, furniture & fixture design, surface finishes, lighting, flooring, sensory detailing, brand touchpoints

A bride arrives carrying more than excitement. There's the weight of choosing what's just right, the overwhelm of too many voices, too many options. She wants her family's blessings; their suggestions shape what she chooses. She's modern and rooted at once — travelling the world styled graciously in her grandmother's heirlooms.

The physical space for Rivaah, Tanishq's premium bridal jewellery brand, needed to hold all of this: her clarity and her questions, her family's presence and her need for quiet, her desire for tradition and her eye for what feels fresh.

It needed to feel elevated without feeling overwhelming, celebratory without being loud — and create space for her voice to emerge.

Design Philosophy

Designed for stillness.
For the moment before certainty arrives.

The palette stays muted — soft greys, warm whites, dusty rose. Nothing competes with what truly shines: her jewellery, her joy, her certainty when she finds it.

Textures play the role here: lime wash walls that feel cool under your palm, copper that warms as light shifts, fabrics that move like breeze, surfaces that invite touch and hold the quiet weight of heritage.

The journey unfolds in chapters. An entrance that slows time. A gallery that invites wandering. Intimate floors that create pockets of privacy within a family affair.

Each space asks a different question: What calls to you? What feels like yours? How do you want to be seen?

Design Interventions

A — Arrival as Ritual

An arch that suggests heritage without insisting on it

The facade greets you first — natural white stone, warm copper columns framing an arch that suggests heritage without demanding it. The street outside is South Ex at full pitch: busy, purposeful, unmissable. And yet.

At dusk, light pools at the entrance like an invitation, warm and patient. Your pace begins to slow. The busy streets fade, and you feel a calm drawing you in — the first intervention before you've even crossed the threshold.

The arch that doesn't announce heritage — it simply remembers it

B — A Moment to Shift

Step through, and you're invited to pause

The foyer opens unhurried. A mannequin styled in full bridal elegance sets the tone. Above, the bougainvillea chandelier by Glass Forest — customised in dusty pink and warm grey to echo Rivaah's palette — holds a subtle celebration without announcing it. You're invited to sit, sip, to let the rhythm of the day set itself.

The foyer before a customer decides what kind of afternoon this will be

C — Gallery as Discovery

The ground floor opens like a question

What draws me in? What feels like mine? Display cases curve through the space, each holding karigari from a different region of India — hands that have shaped metal for generations. Plants soften edges.

The modern bride has travelled the world; she returns with a blend of traditions she's chosen and the ones she's inherited. Here, clarity doesn't come from seeing everything — it comes from discovering what speaks quietly to you.

Not every piece calls to every person. The gallery trusts the jewellery to do its own choosing.

D — Intimacy by Design

Upstairs, the experience grows quieter, closer

Each floor begins with a focal display of what it offers, then divides into pockets — seating large enough for a family, sheer curtains that filter light while maintaining privacy, planters that create intimacy without isolation. Jewellery arrives at your counter. Conversations unfold without rush. Time belongs to you here.

E — Rooms for What Matters

Space for what a wedding actually needs

Long conversations. Trials that take their time. Video calls with the sister who couldn't make it. The private rooms hold the weight of real decision-making — not just selection, but becoming.

Trial rooms have full-length mirrors and room to move — you see yourself in context, wearing the outfit you'll actually wear, making the choice feel real before it is. A stylist understands that customisation isn't about luxury; it's about honouring the bride you imagined as a little girl and the woman you've become.

F — Presence in the Details

The experience lives in what you almost don't notice

Contemporary miniature paintings reinterpreted with modern sensibility. Gesso work by traditional artisans adorning walls like heirlooms you're meeting for the first time. Everything here knows how to wait, how to make space, how to celebrate without announcing itself.

A cinnamon fragrance with hints of burgundy. Soft music carrying Indian instruments through elegant phrasing. The staircase walls shimmer with fabric sandwiched between glass, catching light as you move between floors.

Fabric sandwiched in glass — the shimmer of things that move when you're not looking

The bag you carry home

Behind the Scenes

The fun is in the process

It was the moments that made the space — unhurried, often unseen — where the real excitement of designing our new identity lived. Watching everyone tickle their brains for unexpected oops moments, the bond that built over team dinners and joy of sharing a common pride made this process incredibly fun.
