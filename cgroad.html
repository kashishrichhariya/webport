Celebrating Gujarat:
From Kutch to Ahmedabad

Experience design for Tanishq's Ahmedabad flagship inspired by the state's diverse culture.

Scroll
Project
Tanishq CG Road, Ahmedabad
Year of Completion
2023
Key Contributions
Lead Spatial Designer — experience design and cultural narrative for key feature spaces including jaali partition, cash counters across three floors, chandelier, craft integration, and brand touchpoints.

Gujarat's history of trade has made it uniquely diverse. For centuries, it has welcomed different communities to weave their own traditions into the local Gujarati fabric. CG Road is the beating heart of Ahmedabad where all these worlds meet.

The Tanishq store had to capture this exact legacy— a modern, shared stage where every community belongs.

The Thinking

Where architecture and craft blends beauty with purpose.

Gujarat has a legacy of turning environmental challenges into architectural masterpieces. From the glowing lippan interiors of Kutch to the mathematical precision of its stepwells, every local craft blends profound beauty with utility. Our moodboard is deeply rooted in this shared ethos, an attempt to celebrate this union of craft, purpose, and community identity.

The store spans across 12,000 sqft on three floors, with everyday wear and diamond jewellery on the ground and first floors, and wedding jewellery on the second. Key spaces of customer interaction were identified to create impact through cultural narrative.

Partition Around Staircase

Turning a design conflict into an opportunity

The existing layout around the staircase presented a critical design conflict. Without a clear division, the exposed staircase interfered with the display area. We aimed to turn this challenge into an opportunity by clearly dividing the zones — ensuring a pleasant experience for both staff and customers.

The staircase before intervention — exposed and interfering with display

Jaali partitions were utilised to divide the space while maintaining visual openness. Multiple options were explored taking inspiration from different cultures across the city.

Left: 3D render of the staircase zone. Right: design explorations inspired by Ahmedabad's cultural diversity

The final design is a culmination of regional heritage, blending Jaali patterns from across Ahmedabad with traditional Sankheda spindles. Wood-carved brackets anchor the partitions, reflecting the historic facades of the city's pols and creating an inviting entrance display.

The final jaali partition — Sankheda spindles meet Ahmedabad's pol facades

Ground Floor Cash Counter

The spirit of Gujarat's stepwells

Vavs or stepwells are the most celebrated spaces of Gujarat's heritage, both in the past as gathering spaces, and in the present as a backdrop for wedding shoots. The ground floor counter backdrop carries that spirit. Limewash texture paint lends the space a warm, afternoon glow.

Shallow relief branding in the geometric motifs of Kutch wood carving

Taking center stage in the branding is shallow relief work defined by the geometric motifs of Kutch wood carving. Handcrafted by Jamal ji, this piece beautifully represents the mathematical precision and artistic excellence of the craft.

First Floor Cash Counter

Ahmedabad's iconic tree of life

The Sidi Saiyyed Jaali is Ahmedabad's most iconic image, known for its organic, breathing tree of life. For this space, artisan Abdul ji hand-carved a bespoke piece where the brand logo is seamlessly woven into the vines. The piece is a quiet declaration of belongingness and new beginnings.

The brand logo woven into the tree of life — hand-carved by artisan Abdul ji

Second Floor Cash Counter

Celebrating Ahmedabad's pol architecture

The wedding floor celebrates Ahmedabad's unique pol architecture, featuring floral wood carvings inspired by Mangaldas ni Haveli and classic facade carvings. A pink limewash backdrop completes the space with a warm, celebratory glow.

Lippan work by Ahmedabad-based artist Vishal ji of Lippan Kutir beautifully adapts this historic mud and mirror craft for a contemporary environment. Custom brass and etched-glass lamps warmly echo the traditional lighting of historic havelis.

Lippan craft by Vishal ji of Lippan Kutir — mud and mirror work adapted for contemporary retail

The branding proudly features intricate Sadeli wood inlay by National Awardee Rakesh Petigara ji. With only two practicing artisans remaining in India, highlighting this mathematically precise craft is a crucial step toward its preservation.

Sadeli wood inlay by National Awardee Rakesh Petigara ji — one of only two practicing artisans in India
