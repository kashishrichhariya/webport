If Jaipur stands at the crossroads of heritage and global appeal, MI Road sits at its centre. Tanishq's flagship here had to belong to the historic streetscape while resonating with the woman of today. The project explored designing spaces of interaction with a blend of tradition and contemporary living.

The challenge was to create a space both familiar and contemporary- where the modern woman sees herself reflected.

About the Site

MI Road — The Modern Face of Heritage Jaipur

Connecting Johri Bazaar to Jaipur's newer districts, MI Road marks the transition between the city's historic core and its modern identity. Designed as a colonial boulevard in the 20th century, it features contemporary labels within historic premises.

Design Interventions

The Facade

Flushed in shade of pink
that belongs to the city

Located on heritage MI Road, the facade was designed to belong. The design features arches borrowed from the building's existing design, washed in Jaipur pink- a colour long tied to the city's spirit of hospitality. The real challenge: finding the right shade of pink by testing swatches against centuries-old walls.

Jaipur pink isn't just one shade- it's a gentle blush weathered by history. After testing swatches against the Hawa Mahal, the perfect hue now glows on MI Road.

Existing columns were retained and woven into the new design language. Alongside them, double-height glazing frames the foyer, turning the storefront into a glimpse of the grandeur inside.

The Foyer

An imaginary Jaipur, clad in whites

Stepping inside, the expressive pink exterior transitions into a monochrome canvas. Reimagined in white, the space becomes a quiet celebration of the intricate carvings, royal architecture, and glistening thikri work of the city- creating a grand yet inviting feeling.

At the foyer's centre stands the focal display- inspired by the elevated chabutras in fort gardens, where royal women would gather with their sahelis, spending evenings in intimate circles. The thikri backdrop, handcrafted by artists from Udaipur, glistens in the sunlight, reminiscing the grandeur of Sheesh Mahal in a modern setting.

Above the display area, jharokha windows create palatial layering, their jaali screens casting delicate light and shadow play. Crystal pendant chandeliers cascade through the space like suspended jewels, their faceted surfaces reflecting sunlight from the facade.

The Cash Desk — Ground Floor

Textures of Rajasthani architecture

The double-height cashdesk speaks in the vocabulary of Rajasthani carving. Jaalis and arched openings catch a quiet wash of mood light- and the everyday act of a transaction borrows a little grandeur.

At the cashdesk, the branding is elevated through the craft of Tarkashi. Handcrafted by National Award-winning artist Yogesh Sharma, this technique of inlaying brass wire into wood introduces patterns that shimmer with a subtle, elegant warmth.

The Wedding Floor

A glorious wedding affair

Heading up to the wedding floor, the vibe changes completely from quiet and calm to warm and festive. It's a perfect tribute to Jaipur, a city known for its grand, vibrant, and rich Indian weddings.

The cash counter is rich with layered detail, its jaali and arch patterns adorned with fountain motifs drawn from Jaipur's palace frescoes- symbols of abundance and paradise. Warm-toned lamps cast a golden glow across the space, while the Tanishq branding is rendered in meenakari, the city's signature enamel work in celebratory red and gold.

At the heart of the floor, a mannequin case displays a traditional Jaipuri poshak paired with heritage jewellery. It captures the bride as she's imagined here- rooted in Rajput tradition, dressed for her most significant day, where every layer of fabric and ornament carries meaning and belonging.

A pink textured wall showcases Rajput miniature paintings depicting the solah shringar—the sixteen adornments of a bride. Each frame captures a ritual, from mehndi to jewellery, celebrating the traditions that have adorned brides for generations.
